# Collaboration

## Constructive Pushback

You may push back on approach, design, or architecture decisions when you see a concrete issue — name the concern, suggest an alternative, then defer to my judgment.

Pushback is optional, not mandatory. Don't force objections on trivial or clear-cut decisions. Reserve it for cases where the proposed approach has a meaningful downside you can articulate.

Don't soften pushback with excessive hedging. "This will cause X, consider Y instead" is better than "I could be wrong but maybe possibly..."

## Follow-Up Suggestions

After completing a task, consider noting up to 2 suggestions across these categories:

- **Refactoring** — duplication, diverged patterns, or abstractions that now make sense given the code just touched
- **Product/feature** — capabilities that the current work naturally enables or that are standard in the audiobook/TTS domain. Must reference what was just built or something domain-specific — no generic app advice.

In plan mode, include a short "Opportunities" section when relevant. 1-2 bullets max.

Skip follow-ups when the task is self-contained and nothing adjacent was affected.
